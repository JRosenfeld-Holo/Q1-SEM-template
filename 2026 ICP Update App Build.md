# How to use this document

# **How to Use ICP, Buyer Personas, and Customer Profile**

| Document | Answers | Primary Use Case |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **ICP** | **Which companies** should we target? | Prospecting, targeting, qualification |
| **Buyer Personas** | **Which people** in those companies do we engage? | Messaging, content, outreach strategy |
| **Customer Profile** | **How do successful customers** behave and expand? | Retention, expansion, product development |

## **When to Use Each Document**

### **Use the ICP When You Need To:**

✅ **Build a prospecting list**

* Filter companies by firmographics (size, revenue, funding)  
* Target specific verticals (security, healthcare, smart buildings)  
* Identify companies with right tech stack  
* Score accounts by fit (80-100 \= high priority)

✅ **Qualify inbound leads**

* Does this company match our criteria?  
* Do they have expansion potential?  
* Should we pursue or decline?

✅ **Plan ABM campaigns**

* Which accounts to target  
* What companies to advertise to  
* Where to focus sales resources

✅ **Set ad targeting parameters**

* LinkedIn: Company size, industry, tech stack

**Example**: "We want to build a list of 500 high-fit SMB accounts for Q2 outbound" → Use ICP firmographic criteria \+ ICP fit scoring model

---

### **Use Buyer Personas When You Need To:**

✅ **Craft outreach messages**

* What pain points resonate with VP Engineering?  
* How to speak to Head of Product vs. IoT Manager?  
* Which messaging angles work for each role?

✅ **Create content**

* What blogs should we write for CTOs?  
* What case studies do product leaders want?  
* What technical docs do engineering managers need?

✅ **Plan sales strategy**

* Who should we contact first?  
* How do we multi-thread the account?  
* What questions will each persona ask?

✅ **Handle objections**

* VP Engineering cares about: Reliability, pricing stability  
* Head of Product cares about: Time to market, NPS impact  
* VP Operations cares about: Predictable costs, operational simplicity

**Example**: "How should I approach this Series B company where the CTO responded to our outreach?" → Use VP Engineering/CTO persona to understand their priorities, pain points, and buying concerns

---

### **Use Customer Profile When You Need To:**

✅ **Assess customer health**

* Is this customer growing or at-risk?  
* What signals indicate expansion readiness?  
* Should we intervene to prevent churn?

✅ **Identify expansion opportunities**

* Which customers are ready for upsell?  
* What triggers expansion conversations?  
* How to drive organic device adds?

✅ **Build product roadmap**

* What features drive product-led growth?  
* What capabilities enable self-serve expansion?  
* Where are customers getting stuck?

✅ **Improve onboarding**

* What predicts successful activation?  
* How fast should first device deploy?  
* What milestones matter most?

**Example**: "This customer has been at 50 devices for 6 months with no growth. What should we do?" → Use Customer Profile "Yellow/At-Risk" health signals to diagnose issue and plan intervention

**SMB companies represent a selective, high-potential pipeline for Mid-Market and Enterprise growth.**

* **74%** of all deals by volume (high-volume segment)

### **🎯 Highest Priority: Video Telematics & AI-Enabled Video**

## **Firmographic Criteria**

### **Company Size**

**Employee Count:**

* **Target**: 10-100 employees  
* **Sweet Spot**: 20-50 employees (growth stage)  
* **Consider**: \>100 employees (likely Mid-Market, different ICP)  
* **Avoid**: \<10 employees (too early, resource-constrained)

**Why This Matters:**

* Companies at 20-50 employees are in active scaling mode  
* Large enough to have budget and deployment capacity  
* Small enough to move quickly without enterprise procurement

### **Revenue & Funding**

**Annual Revenue:**

* **Target**: $2M-$20M ARR  
* **Sweet Spot**: $5M-$15M (product-market fit achieved, scaling)  
* **Avoid**: \<$1M (too early stage, price-sensitive)

**Funding Stage:**

* ✅ **Series A or later** (raised $5M+)  
* ✅ **Bootstrapped with traction** (\>$5M ARR, profitable)  
* ✅ **Post-product-market fit** with proven growth trajectory  
* ❌ **Pre-seed/Seed** (\<$2M raised, no revenue traction)  
* ❌ **Bootstrapped without traction** (\<$1M ARR, struggling)

**Growth Trajectory:**

* **Target**: 20%+ YoY growth

## **Behavioral & Environmental Signals**

### **Trigger Events (Buy Signals)**

**High-Priority Triggers:**

1. **Surprise price increase from current carrier/MVNO**  
   * Example quotes: "T-Mobile tried to jack rates up double" (Eagle Eye)  
   * "35% increase, now another large increase" (Wattwatchers)  
   * **Action**: Immediate outreach with pricing stability message  
2. **Video product launch or expansion**  
* Company announces fleet video product, AI-powered cameras, or autonomous robot launch  
* Press releases mentioning "video telematics", "AI video analytics", "computer vision"  
* **Action**: Position as high-bandwidth video connectivity partner  
3. **Insurance mandate for fleet video**  
   * Fleet customers facing insurance requirements for dash cams  
   * Industry news about insurance discounts for video-equipped fleets  
   * **Action:** "We support fleet video providers with reliable, high-bandwidth connectivity"  
4. **Recent funding round** (Series A or later)  
   * Indicates budget availability and growth mandate  
   * Often comes with geographic/product expansion plans  
   * **Action**: Pitch as infrastructure for scale  
5. **Hiring surge in Engineering/Product**  
   * Signals product expansion, new features, scaling  
   * Indicates capacity for technical integration  
   * **Action**: Lead with developer-friendly positioning  
6. **Geographic expansion announcement**  
   * Moving from US → Canada, US → International, etc.  
   * Indicates need for multi-region coverage  
   * **Action**: Emphasize global carrier relationships  
7. **New product launch or use case**  
   * Launching new device types  
   * Expanding into new verticals  
   * **Action**: Position as flexible, multi-use-case platform  
8. **Acquisition or merger**  
   * Consolidating connectivity vendors  
   * Standardizing across combined entity  
   * **Action**: Pitch unified platform consolidation

**Medium-Priority Triggers:**

* Moving to cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP migration)  
* Regulatory compliance requirements (HIPAA, SOC2, etc.)  
* Customer expansion milestones (10K→50K+ users)  
* Partnership announcements with large enterprises  
* **Video-specific**: AI/ML model deployment, edge computing infrastructure updates

### **Negative Signals (Disqualifiers)**

❌ **BYO SIM preference** (50%+ of fleet on BYO model)

* **Why**: Structural misalignment with our model

❌ **Price-only evaluation** (no technical requirements discussion)

* Only question: "What's your cheapest plan?"  
* **Why**: Will churn at first competitive offer

❌ **No scalability potential** (\<100 devices, no growth plan)

* Static deployment, no expansion roadmap  
* **Why**: CAC not justified by LTV

❌ **Consumer-adjacent use cases & travel SIMs**

* Targeting end consumers, not B2B/B2B2C  
* **Why**: Different economics, different support needs

❌ **Developer-only engagement** (no executive sponsorship)

* Only technical team evaluating, no budget holder  
* **Why**: Unlikely to close without executive buy-in

### **Decision-Making Process**

### **Objection Handling**

**Objection \#1: "We're already locked into a contract"**

* **Response**: "When does your contract renew? Most enterprise customers start evaluating 6-12 months before renewal. We can run a parallel pilot so you're ready to switch when the contract expires."  
* **Real example**: Eagle Eye evaluated Hologram while still under T-Mobile contract after price increase announcement

**Objection \#2: "Switching providers is too risky/disruptive"**

* **Response**: "That's exactly why we offer phased migrations. Start with new deployments, prove reliability, then gradually migrate existing fleet. Reference: \[Customer X\] migrated 10,000 devices with zero downtime over 6 months."  
* **Real example**: Wattwatchers migrating 5-6K new devices annually to new provider

**Objection \#3: "We need \[advanced feature\] you don't have"**

* **Response**: "Tell me more about the use case. We might have it on our roadmap or have another way to solve it. What's driving that requirement?"  
* **Follow-up**: If truly a gap, assess if it's a must-have or nice-to-have. Can we build it? Can customer wait?

**Objection \#4: "Your competitor is cheaper"**

* **Response**: "I understand cost matters for enterprise budgets. Let me ask: have you experienced surprise price increases from providers before? Most customers we work with prioritize total cost of ownership and pricing stability over the lowest price today. What matters more to you—cheapest price upfront or predictable costs over 3 years?"

**Objection \#5: "We need to see \[specific customer\] as a reference"**

* **Response**: "Great question. While I can't share customer names without permission, I can connect you with \[similar company in same vertical\] who's deploying at similar scale. What specific questions do you want answered?"

## **Expansion Triggers & Timing**

### **Internal Trigger Events**

**Product Launch** (New use case)

* **Timing**: Expansion within 3-6 months of launch  
* **Signal**: Press release, product page, hiring for new vertical  
* **Action**: "How can we support your new product with connectivity?"

**QBR**

* **Timing**: Quarterly  
* **Signal**: Customer reaching expansion milestones ($5K, $10K MRR, 1,000 devices, Consistent MRR growth (3+ months of 10%+ monthly growth), Executive engagement increasing, Multiple use cases or geographies deployed  
* **Action**: "Let’s discuss volume discounts, new features, and a multi-year contract that supports your growth.”

**Funding Round**

* **Timing**: Expansion within 6-12 months of funding  
* **Signal**: TechCrunch announcement, LinkedIn updates  
* **Action**: "Congratulations on the funding. Let's discuss scaling your connectivity infrastructure."

**Geographic Expansion**

* **Timing**: Immediate (new geography \= new connectivity needs)  
* **Signal**: Job postings in new region, market announcements  
* **Action**: "We have coverage in \[new market\]. Let's ensure smooth deployment."

**Acquisition/Merger**

* **Timing**: 6-18 months post-close (integration period)  
* **Signal**: M\&A announcement  
* **Action**: "We can help consolidate connectivity across the merged entity."

### **External Trigger Events**

**Competitor Price Increase**

* **Timing**: Immediate (pain is fresh)  
* **Signal**: Inbound inquiry, social media complaints  
* **Action**: "We've helped companies switch from \[competitor\] with pricing stability guarantees."

**Carrier Coverage Issues**

* **Timing**: Immediate (active pain point)  
* **Signal**: Support tickets, device offline complaints  
* **Action**: "Multi-carrier switching can eliminate coverage gaps."

**Regulatory Changes**

* **Timing**: 3-12 months (compliance cycles)  
* **Signal**: Industry news, regulatory filings  
* **Action**: "Our SOC2/HIPAA compliance can help you meet new requirements."

**Market Consolidation**

* **Timing**: Ongoing  
* **Signal**: Competitors going out of business  
* **Action**: "We're a stable, growing partner for the long term."

## **Retention & Churn Patterns**

### **What Predicts Retention (Stays 3+ years)**

**Product Usage:**

* Regular platform engagement  
* API integration (automated management)  
* Multiple use cases or device types  
* Self-serve expansion (organic growth)

**Business Factors:**

* Product-market fit (customers' product is successful)  
* Executive sponsorship (not just engineering)  
* Strategic vendor positioning (partnership mindset)  
* Multi-year contract (commitment signal)

**Support Experience:**

* Few support tickets (self-sufficient)  
* Quick resolution when issues arise  
* Proactive CSM engagement

**Expansion Behavior:**

* Steady organic MRR growth  
* At least one expansion event (deal or major device add)  
* Geographic or use case expansion

### **What Predicts Churn**

**Product Usage:**

* Declining device count  
* No platform logins (30+ days)  
* Never integrated API (manual management only)  
* Single use case, no expansion

**Business Factors:**

* Customer's product failing (market fit issues)  
* No executive awareness (engineering-only relationship)  
* Transactional mindset (price-only focus)  
* Month-to-month contract only

**Support Experience:**

* Frequent support tickets (struggling)  
* Unresolved issues accumulating  
* No CSM relationship  
* Negative feedback

**Expansion Behavior:**

* Flat or declining MRR  
* No expansion events in 12+ months  
* No roadmap for growth  
* Mentions competitor evaluation

### **Churn Prevention Playbook**

**90 Days Before Renewal:**

* Executive outreach with renewal proposal  
* Review usage and value delivered  
* Address any outstanding concerns  
* Offer renewal incentives if needed

**At-Risk Signal Detection:**

* Weekly monitoring of usage metrics  
* Monthly health score review  
* Quarterly CSM check-ins  
* Immediate escalation on red signals

**Win-Back Strategy (If Churned):**

* Understand root cause of churn  
* 90-day wait before re-engagement  
* New champion identification  
* Competitive win-back offer if applicable

### **6.2 Methodology Notes**

**How We Measured Expansion:**

**Deal-Level:**

* "Expansion" \= deals marked as "Existing Business"  
* "Multiple deals" \= accounts with 2+ closed-won deals  
* "PARR growth" \= difference between first and last deal PARR

**Customer-Level:**

* "Expansion" \= MRR growth of \>$100 from first to last month  
* "MRR growth" \= last month MRR minus first month MRR  
* "Months active" \= number of months with positive MRR

**Segmentation Classification:**

* **SMB**: Initial PARR \<$20K OR current MRR suggests \<$20K annual  
* **Mid-Market**: Initial PARR $20K-$100K  
* **Enterprise**: Initial PARR \>$100K

**Important**: Customers classified as "SMB" in the data may have since graduated to Mid-Market or Enterprise. The segment reflects their *starting* size, not current size.
